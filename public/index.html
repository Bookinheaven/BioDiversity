About Biodiversity

Biodiversity represents the variety and variability of life on Earth. It includes the diversity of species, ecosystems, and genetic variations within species. Biodiversity plays a crucial role in maintaining ecosystem balance and resilience.

It supports everything in nature we need to survive: food, clean water, medicine, and shelter. Losing biodiversity means losing these vital resources.

Biodiversity Through Time

Prehistoric Life

Life began in the oceans 3.5 billion years ago, leading to diverse microbial life.

Age of Dinosaurs

The Mesozoic era brought incredible biodiversity, especially among reptiles and plants.

Rise of Mammals

After the extinction of dinosaurs, mammals evolved and adapted to fill ecological niches.

Modern Era

Modern biodiversity includes millions of species, but faces threats from human activity.
